Project dossier · SN9 / IOTA

IOTA — Macrocosmos Bittensor Subnet 9

Decentralized model training over heterogeneous, permissionless hardware. This is Macrocosmos SN9, not the separate IOTA L1.

Overview

IOTA coordinates model layers, activations and weight updates across open-internet miners while validators score useful contribution. The underwriting question is whether the system can deliver centralized-grade training at lower cost and turn that into durable paid demand for SN9.

Overview

  • IOTA here is Macrocosmos' Incentivised Orchestrated Training Architecture, Bittensor Subnet 9—not the separate IOTA L1.
  • An orchestrator assigns model layers; miners process activations and update weights; validators spot-check contribution quality and direct subnet rewards.
  • Primary evidence surfaces: IOTA dashboard, open-source repository, Train at Home, technical paper and Project Orion / Orion-100B artifacts.

Hermes take

2026-07-15 — SN9 is one of the few decentralized-training bets with a real technical surface: open code, a live dashboard, consumer participation and a concrete 100B-scale target. The setup matters if Orion publishes reproducible quality, speed and cost while Train at Home retains contributors without overpaying emissions. Until then, “65% of centralized speed” is a strong demo claim—not a business. Watch paid training demand and cost per useful token.

Token / contract notes

  • Exposure is the Bittensor SN9 alpha asset and pool, not the unrelated IOTA L1 token and not an ERC-20/SPL contract.
  • Track alpha price, pool depth, emissions, validator stake and miner rewards through canonical Bittensor data. Alpha pool value is not normal equity value or FDV.
  • No separate IOTA project token/contract was documented in the official sources reviewed.
  • Training progress does not by itself prove external paid demand, sustainable rewards or value capture net of emissions.

Risks

  • Issuer-reported speed, convergence and cost need independent reproduction.
  • A distributed miner fleet can still depend on centralized scheduling, data, checkpoints, reward logic or validators.
  • Emissions can recruit hardware without proving external buyers or positive unit economics.
  • WAN latency, churn and stragglers can erase cheap-hardware savings at scale.
  • Spot checks and contribution scoring must resist corrupt activations, collusion and gaming.
  • SN9 alpha pool structure creates liquidity/reflexivity risk and must not be confused with IOTA L1.

Open questions

  • Can third parties reproduce Orion-100B quality, 65%-of-centralized speed and total cost per useful training token?
  • Who pays for training, how many runs are externally funded, and what are repeat demand, revenue and gross margin?
  • How many Train at Home devices remain active by cohort, and how concentrated are useful work and rewards?
  • How decentralized are orchestration, data, validators, checkpoint custody and upgrades?
  • What are measured false-acceptance/rejection rates for malicious or corrupt work?
  • How do emissions, stake and alpha liquidity compare with delivered training and external revenue?
  • When does the next Orion artifact ship, and will it include code, evals and cost data?
