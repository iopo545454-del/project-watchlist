Overview

  • Bittensor Subnet 64 / SN64.
  • Decentralized serverless AI compute and managed inference platform.
  • Best direct watchlist comp for open-model inference demand because it already serves large open models, including GLM-5.2 in recent scans.
  • Alpha token: SN64 within Bittensor dTAO economy.

Latest scan notes

  • 2026-07-23 — dstack merged private-ai-gateway PR #101, publishing the exact measured app_compose preimage in ACI evidence and verifying it against the RTMR3-bound Compose hash. Chutes is a supported verified upstream, so its gateway workload manifest can now be independently hash-checked; reviewed source/image acceptance, production deployment and Chutes-attributed traffic remain unproven. Source: official merge commit.
  • 2026-07-22 — Chutes said the Harvard research data-collection phase ends July 24: the opt-in endpoint, 25% inference discount and prompt/response recording will end, while users return to the primary API with unchanged keys. Endpoint-attributed traffic and revenue are not public, so the post-closure effect remains a KPI check. Source: official migration notice.
  • 2026-07-22 — Dedicated verification closed the revenue-quality / TEE / Parallax check as confirmed with bounds. Seven complete days through July 21 showed $96,179.17 revenue—79.85% paygo, 17.58% subscriptions, 2.58% private instances and 0% in the explicit sponsored field—against 331.007B tokens, or $290.6K/T; the 30-day ratio was $275.8K/T. This supports the published 90-day order of magnitude but not the $340K July 20 point. Provider margin and dstack/ChronoSeek-attributed volume remain undisclosed; the Parallax report lacks code/checkpoints, replication and paid-job economics. Sources: revenue mix, throughput, dstack integration and Parallax report.

The recap also says dstack's private-ai-gateway routes to Chutes after GPU TEE attestation and ChronoSeek uses private-subnet support; organic mix, gross margin and partner-attributed volume remain undisclosed. Sources: official recap and live revenue.

The linked draft report gives validation loss 3.2521 at 50k baseline-equivalent steps and best exported median 3.2104, but explicitly lacks replicated equivalence tests and measured wall-clock speedups. No code/checkpoint, paid job or SN64 training-economics loop is live. Sources: official post, report link and draft report.

Thesis

  • Chutes is the cleanest decentralized neo-cloud investment case because it combines direct inference demand, model-serving product-market fit, and public revenue data.
  • It is closer to a decentralized OpenRouter/DeepInfra/Fireworks-style managed inference layer than a raw GPU rental marketplace.
  • If open-source model usage compounds and routers need cheaper backend capacity, Chutes should be one of the first Bittensor subnets to show it in revenue.

Open questions

  • The known Harvard 25% research discount ends July 24; what other off-ledger credits/rebates/incentives remain, and does post-closure revenue per token change?
  • What are provider payouts/expenses and retained gross margin?
  • Which integrations route recurring volume? dstack routing and measured-Compose verification are code-confirmed, but live Chutes deployment evidence and partner-attributed volume are not public.
  • How stable is model quality/latency versus centralized inference providers?
  • Parallax's paid-economics follow-up was closed stale on 2026-07-22; reopen only for code/checkpoints, independent replication or paid-job revenue.
